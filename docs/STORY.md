# Story & Narrative Design

_The narrative bible. The spine of the whole project: a soul reassembling its own
existence across supernatural worlds — **spirit, bones, and flesh.**_

> This document is the canonical story design. It captures the intended arc
> faithfully; the final **"How the story maps to the systems"** section is the
> integration layer that ties it to what's actually built (see
> [GAME_VISION.md](GAME_VISION.md), [CURRENT_STATE.md](CURRENT_STATE.md)).

## Logline

The player's journey follows the gradual reconstruction of their existence:
**spirit → bones → flesh.** A fragmented soul must escape the depths of an
afterlife, travel through many supernatural realms, recover its scattered body,
reach the living world, and uncover the truth of its own death.

The structure can ship as **one large game in three acts**, **three campaigns in
one title**, or a **trilogy** of standalone games — decided later (see *Release
Structure*).

## The realms

The universe is **not centered exclusively on Hell**. The player travels through
multiple supernatural worlds inspired by different mythologies, folklore, and
cosmic horror. Each realm has its own creatures, visual identity, environmental
rules, hazards, bosses, cards, and narrative clues. Despite their differences,
all realms are connected to the player's death, fragmented identity, and attempt
to become physically whole again.

Potential realm inspirations:

- H.P. Lovecraft and cosmic horror
- Japanese horror mythology & folklore — Yuki-onna, Tengu, Oni, Bakemono and
  other supernatural creatures
- Underworlds and afterlife realms from different cultures
- Possible purgatorial dimensions
- A possible Fey realm
- Additional mythological worlds introduced throughout progression

## Phase One — *Raise That Specter*  (Spirit)

The player begins as a **disembodied ghost** trapped in the depths of an
afterlife realm. This opening may *resemble* Hell, but the phase does not remain
in Hell or revolve around traditional demonic imagery. After escaping the depths,
the player travels through different mythological and supernatural realms
(Lovecraftian dimensions, Japanese horror-inspired regions, other afterlife or
nightmare realms).

The player has **no physical body** — only a soul, ghost, or spectral
consciousness.

**Objectives**
- Escape the deepest region of the afterlife.
- Learn to survive as a spectral being.
- Travel between supernatural worlds.
- Discover why the soul has not disappeared.
- Search for a path back toward physical existence.
- Gather clues about the former life and death.

**The mystery (partial).** Phase One does *not* fully reveal why the player died.
It provides fragmented memories, environmental clues, cryptic dialogue, visions &
nightmares, symbolic imagery, conflicting accounts of the past, beings that seem
to recognize the player, and allusions to a greater event tied to the death.
Clues create *theories without complete answers* — some intentionally ambiguous
so the mystery can carry into later phases. The player may not even remember their
own identity at first: sensations, relationships, locations, and moments return
without the connections between them.

**Spectral mechanics** (gameplay reflects the bodiless condition)
- Passing through specific barriers
- Possessing objects or weakened creatures
- Spectral movement abilities; ethereal dodges / short-range teleportation
- Reduced interaction with physical objects
- Spirit-based attacks
- Resistance to certain physical attacks
- Vulnerability to spiritual bindings, rituals, and supernatural weapons
- Abilities that manipulate fear, memory, or souls

**Cards** in this phase represent lost memories, spiritual contracts, supernatural
knowledge, or fragments of power taken from the realms.

**End state.** The player escapes the deepest layers of the afterlife but remains
incomplete: soul and identity preserved, still no physical form.

## Phase Two — *Roll the Bones*  (Bones)

The player continues through mythological and supernatural realms, possibly
including **Purgatory, a Fey realm, forgotten underworlds, dream dimensions,
abandoned spiritual kingdoms, and mythological borderlands** between life and
death. (Purgatory and the Fey realm are possibilities, not confirmed.)

**Primary objective:** locate the **scattered bones** of the player's original
body. The bones may have been separated intentionally, hidden across realms,
claimed by powerful creatures, used in rituals, transformed into sacred or cursed
artifacts, guarded by bosses, scattered during the death event, or taken by an
unknown individual or faction.

As bones are recovered, the ghost gradually **inhabits and reconstructs its
skeleton.** Recovery drives *gameplay*, not just story collection:

| Bone recovered | Gameplay unlock |
|---|---|
| Leg bones | Stronger movement / sprinting |
| Foot bones | Dodges, jumps, traversal abilities |
| Arm bones | Heavier weapons / physical attacks |
| Hand bones | Extra card interactions / equipment |
| Rib bones | Health / defense |
| Spine | New ability slots / physical control |
| Skull | Restored memories, perception, advanced abilities |
| Jaw | Speech, dialogue choices, supernatural commands |

**Appearance evolves visibly:** early Phase Two, a ghost carrying or inhabiting a
few skeletal pieces; by the end, a full skeleton animated by the soul.

**Mystery deepens.** More significant information surfaces, but the complete
explanation is withheld. Recovered bones may carry memories or spiritual
impressions from the former life — each major recovery reveals a fragment of the
truth while raising new questions.

**End state.** A preserved soul; a complete (or nearly complete) skeleton; greater
control over the physical world; new combat & traversal abilities; a clearer sense
of the former identity; and evidence that the death was **more complicated than it
first appeared**. Still no flesh — no full return to the living world.

---

## Phase Three — *Ashes to Ashes*  (Flesh)

The player reaches the **living world**, or a distorted version of it. Final
objective: **restore flesh and become whole — soul, bones, and body reunited.**

The living world may not be the place the player remembers. Time may have passed
differently across realms; the death may have changed reality. Possible
conditions: the world corrupted; decades or centuries have passed; the player's
home is gone; connected people changed or vanished; a cult or faction benefited
from the death; mythological realms bleeding into the living world; the
resurrection threatening the balance between worlds; someone deliberately
preventing the return.

**Flesh restoration** (tied to major bosses, rituals, memories, or objects from
the former life) can unlock: full weapon handling; armor & equipment; expanded
health/stamina systems; human interactions & new dialogue; physical status
effects; greater offensive power; new vulnerabilities; and abilities that
**combine spectral and physical mechanics**.

**The full reveal.** Phase Three finally resolves the mystery, explaining: who the
player was; what caused the death; whether it was accidental, intentional, or
sacrificial; who/what was responsible; why the body was separated; why the soul
remained active; why the bones were scattered; how the realms connect; why the
player could return; and what consequences resurrection brings. The truth may
reveal the death was tied to a **larger supernatural event**, not an isolated
personal tragedy.

**Becoming whole is not automatically winning.** Full resurrection may force a
final moral/narrative choice, such as:

- Remain human, or become something greater
- Sacrifice the restored body to repair the realms
- Take revenge on those responsible
- Break the cycle of death and resurrection
- Replace a god, ruler, or cosmic entity
- Return to ordinary life carrying supernatural power
- Discover the player *willingly* caused their own death
- Learn that resurrection will release an even greater threat

The conclusion completes the mystery of the death while determining **what the
player becomes** after recovering soul, bones, and flesh.

## Release structure (open)

The three phases support several production formats — undecided for now:

1. **One complete game** — three major acts: spectral → skeletal → resurrected.
2. **Three campaigns in one title** — each phase its own realms, enemies,
   progression, and mechanical identity.
3. **A three-game series (trilogy):**
   1. *Raise That Specter* — the soul escapes the afterlife and travels the realms.
   2. *Roll the Bones* — the soul recovers and inhabits its scattered remains.
   3. *Ashes to Ashes* — the skeleton reaches the living world, regains flesh, and
      uncovers the complete truth.

The trilogy structure lets each release own a central objective while preserving
the larger mystery across all three.

## How the story maps to the systems we've built

_This section is the integration layer — how the narrative rides on the existing
roguelite mechanics. Proposals to confirm, not fixed law._

- **Realms = universes.** The mythos worlds we already model (Lovecraftian Coast,
  Japanese, Norse, and future Fey/underworld/purgatory realms) *are* the realms
  the soul travels. The existing **fate-shaping** universe-selection becomes
  "learning to steer between realms."
- **A ghost can't truly die → permadeath fits perfectly.** Each roguelite run is
  an *attempt* to push deeper / recover a fragment; death casts the incomplete
  soul back, and the **Moment of Recall** is the soul remembering after each
  failed attempt. Reincarnation is reframed: not "become a new person each life,"
  but "the unfinished soul is repelled and tries again, stronger."
- **The clue system = memories, tattoos, and the Recall report.** Fragmented
  memories, cryptic visions, and conflicting accounts are delivered through the
  systems we already have for death reports, memories, and Memory Tattoos —
  drip-fed per death.
- **Progression *is* the body.** The soul/meta-progression axis gains a concrete
  spine:
  - **Phase 1 (spirit):** cards are memories / contracts / knowledge; combat is
    ethereal — freeze-to-plan and spirit attacks, few physical options, strong
    against the physical but vulnerable to bindings/rituals.
  - **Phase 2 (bones):** a **bone-recovery meta-track** extends the body-archetype,
    ability-slot, and Mirror systems — each bone permanently unlocks movement,
    slots, health, or abilities, and the character visibly reconstructs.
  - **Phase 3 (flesh):** the final meta tier — full weapons/armor cards and
    spectral+physical combos.
- **This gives the freeze-to-plan combat a natural power curve** across the whole
  game: you begin barely able to touch the world (ghostly, plan-heavy) and grow
  into a physical, weapon-wielding force — earned through the story, not a stat
  slider.
- **Lovecraftian dread is the connective tissue** — the same unspeakable event
  wearing different cultural masks across realms, and the horror of remembering
  your own death one shard at a time.

## Open alignment questions (for later)

- **Run-death vs. macro arc:** confirm runs are the *texture within* each phase
  while the three phases are one-way campaign progress (spirit→bones→flesh is
  permanent; you don't "reincarnate backward").
- **The vessel:** in Phase 1 you're bodiless — so the current per-life "body
  archetype" concept becomes *phase-driven state* (spectre → partial skeleton →
  skeleton → flesh) rather than a per-run pick. Confirm.
- **Format:** one game vs. three campaigns vs. trilogy — affects how much of Phase
  One must stand alone as a complete first release.
